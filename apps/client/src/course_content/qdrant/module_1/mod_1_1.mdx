# What is a Vector Database?

Until now, we have searched for data using **Keywords** (SQL `LIKE`), **Names** (Mongo `{name: "Alice"}`), or **Connections** (Neo4j `(a)-[:REL]->(b)`).

**Vector Databases** do something different: they search for **Meaning**.

---

## The Concept of "Embeddings"

Computers don't understand words. They understand numbers. An **Embedding** is a mathematical representation of a piece of data (text, image, audio) as a long list of numbers called a **Vector**.

**Example:**

- "King" -> `[0.1, 0.9, 0.4]`
- "Queen" -> `[0.1, 0.85, 0.41]`
- "Pizza" -> `[0.8, 0.1, 0.2]`

Notice how "King" and "Queen" have similar numbers? Thatâ€™s because they share a similar _meaning_. "Pizza" is completely different.

---

## Real-World Example: "I'm looking for a place to sleep."

- **Keyword Search (SQL):** Would look for the exact word "sleep" or "place." It might return nothing.
- **Vector Search (Qdrant):** Understands the _concept_ of sleep. It might return "Hotels," "Hostels," or "Inns," even if the word "sleep" never appears in their description.

---

## Why Qdrant?

Qdrant (pronounced "Quadrant") is a high-performance vector database written in **Rust**. It is designed to store these millions of number-lists and find the closest matches in milliseconds.
